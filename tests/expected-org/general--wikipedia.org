*Obsidian* is a [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Personal_knowledge_management][personal knowledge management]] and [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Note-taking][note-taking]] [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Application_software][software application]] that operates on files. [fn:2] It allows users to make internal links for notes and then to visualize the connections as a graph. It is designed to help users organize and structure their thoughts and knowledge in a flexible, non-linear way. [fn:3] The software is free for personal use, with commercial licenses and hosted services such as [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Obsidian_Publish][Obsidian Publish]] and [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Obsidian_Sync][Obsidian Sync]] available as paid add-ons. [fn:4]

Obsidian is developed by Dynalist Inc., a company founded by [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shida_Li][Shida Li]] and Erica Xu. The application stores notes as plain-text Markdown files in a local folder called a "vault", allowing users full control over their data. [fn:5] It supports a wide range of [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plug-in_(computing)][plugins]] and themes created by its [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open-source_software][open-source]] community. [fn:6]

** History

Obsidian was created by Shida Li and Erica Xu, who previously developed the outliner application [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dynalist][Dynalist]]. [fn:7] The first public version was released in March 2020 as a desktop application for [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Windows][Windows]], [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MacOS][macOS]], and [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linux][Linux]]. [fn:8] The application quickly gained a following among users interested in the [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zettelkasten][Zettelkasten]] method and [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Personal_knowledge_management][personal knowledge management]] workflows. [fn:9]

Mobile versions for [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IOS][iOS]] and [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Android_(operating_system)][Android]] were released in 2021. [fn:10] In October 2022, the application reached version 1.0, which included a new default theme, improved tab support, and a redesigned settings interface. [fn:11]

Obsidian introduced [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Obsidian_Publish][Obsidian Publish]], a paid service for publishing notes as a website, and [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Obsidian_Sync][Obsidian Sync]], an [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/End-to-end_encryption][end-to-end encrypted]] synchronization service. [fn:12]

** Features

Obsidian stores all notes as plain-text files in a local folder on the user's device, referred to as a "vault". [fn:13] This approach gives users complete ownership of their data and allows notes to be opened with any text editor.

*** Linking and graph view

A core feature of Obsidian is its support for [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internal_link][internal links]] between notes using a ~[[wikilink]]~ syntax. [fn:14] These links create a network of connected notes that can be visualized through a built-in graph view. [fn:15]

*** Plugins

Obsidian supports an extensive [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plug-in_(computing)][plugin]] system that allows third-party developers to extend the application's functionality. [fn:16] As of 2024, the community plugin repository contains over 1,500 plugins. [fn:17]

** Reception

Obsidian has received generally favorable reviews from technology publications. /[[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Verge][The Verge]]/ praised the application for its flexibility and local-first approach, noting that "Obsidian is a powerful note-taking app that gives you full control of your data." [fn:2] /[[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_Magazine][PC Magazine]]/ awarded it an Editors' Choice designation, highlighting its linking capabilities and customizability, though noting a steep learning curve for new users. [fn:3]

The application has been compared to other note-taking tools such as [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Notion_(productivity_software)][Notion]], [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roam_Research][Roam Research]], and [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logseq][Logseq]]. Commentators have noted that Obsidian differentiates itself through its local-first storage model and emphasis on user data ownership. [fn:18] However, critics have pointed out that the [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proprietary_software][proprietary]] nature of the application itself limits user freedom compared to fully [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open-source_software][open-source]] alternatives. [fn:19]
